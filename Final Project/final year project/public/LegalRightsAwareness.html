Fundamental Rights (Indian Constitution)

Fundamental Rights are basic human rights guaranteed by the Indian Constitution. These rights apply to all citizens, including students, children, and minors. They protect individuals from unfair treatment and ensure freedom, equality, and dignity in everyday life.

Right to Equality

  • The Right to Equality ensures that everyone is treated equally before the law.
  • No person can be discriminated against on the basis of religion, caste, gender, race, or place of birth.
  • All citizens are equal in the eyes of the law, whether rich or poor, powerful or ordinary.
  • Students and children also enjoy equal opportunities in education, public facilities, and protection.

  • This right promotes fairness and prevents social injustice and discrimination.

Right to Freedom

The Right to Freedom allows individuals to live freely and express themselves.

  • Freedom of speech and expression ‚Äì You can express your opinions, ideas, and thoughts freely.
  • Freedom to assemble peacefully ‚Äì You can participate in peaceful meetings, protests, or gatherings.
  • Freedom to move freely throughout India ‚Äì You can travel or live in any part of the country.

  • Public safety
  • Law and order
  • National security

Right Against Exploitation

This right protects people, especially children, from exploitation and abuse.

  • Child labour in hazardous jobs is strictly prohibited.
  • No one can be forced to work against their will (forced labour).
  • Human trafficking, including trafficking of children, is illegal.
  • This right ensures that children can grow, learn, and live safely.

Right to Freedom of Religion

India is a secular country, and this right guarantees religious freedom.

  • You are free to follow, practice, and respect any religion.
  • No one can force you to follow a religion against your will.
  • All religions are treated equally by the state.
  • This right encourages tolerance and peaceful coexistence.

Right to Education

Education is a fundamental right for children.

  • Free and compulsory education is provided to children aged 6 to 14 years.
  • The government must ensure access to quality education.
  • This right helps children build knowledge, skills, and a better future.
  • Schools must not deny education based on caste, gender, or economic status.

Right to Constitutional Remedies

This right protects all other fundamental rights.

  • If your rights are violated, you can approach the courts.
  • Courts can issue orders to protect your rights.
  • This right ensures justice and accountability.
  • It acts as a safeguard against misuse of power.

Your Rights if Questioned by Police

If a student or minor is questioned or detained by the police, they have special protections.

  • You cannot be detained without a valid reason.
  • You have the right to know why you are being detained.
  • Parents or guardians must be informed immediately.
  • It acts as a safeguard against misuse of power.
  • You cannot be forced to confess or sign statements.
  • These rules protect children from misuse of authority.

Special Rights for Minors (Under 18)

Children are treated differently under the law.

  • Minors are handled under juvenile justice laws, not adult criminal laws.
  • The focus is on care, protection, reform, and rehabilitation, not punishment.
  • Children have the right to be protected from: Abuse, Neglect, Exploitation
  • This approach helps children reform and lead better lives.

Rights at School

Schools must provide a safe and respectful environment.

  • Physical punishment and harassment are strictly prohibited.
  • Students have the right to a safe, healthy learning environment.
  • All students must be treated equally, regardless of background.
  • Schools should promote dignity, discipline, and emotional safety.

Digital & Privacy Rights

In the digital age, students also have online rights.

  • Protection from cyberbullying, online harassment, and abuse.
  • Personal data, photos, and videos cannot be misused without consent.
  • You can report cybercrimes to authorities.
  • These rights protect mental health, privacy, and online safety.

If Your Rights Are Violated

If any of your rights are violated, you should take action.

  • Talk to a trusted adult such as a parent, teacher, or guardian.
  • Contact Childline 1098, a 24/7 helpline for children in India.
  • Report serious issues to: School authorities, Local police, Child protection services
  • Taking action helps protect you and others.
